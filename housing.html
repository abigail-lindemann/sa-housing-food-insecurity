Across Bexar County, nearly half of all renters spend more than 30 percent of their income on housing — the federal threshold for cost burden. In the hardest-hit neighborhoods, that share climbs above 70 percent. Cost burden is not evenly distributed: it concentrates on the East, South, and West sides of San Antonio, in areas with median household incomes below $35,000.

What the map shows

Each block group is shaded by its housing insecurity score — a composite of renter cost burden rate, severe cost burden rate (paying more than 50% of income on housing), and overcrowding rate. Darker red indicates higher insecurity. Click any block group to see its individual scores.

Cost burden and income

The relationship between income and housing cost burden is stark. Block groups with median household incomes below $30,000 show cost burden rates above 60 percent on average. Above $60,000, that rate drops below 25 percent. Housing stress in San Antonio is overwhelmingly concentrated among low-income renters.

Overcrowding

Overcrowding — defined as more than one person per room — affects a smaller share of households but clusters intensely in specific neighborhoods. The highest overcrowding rates appear in areas with large immigrant and Hispanic populations on the West and South sides, where families double up due to housing cost pressure.

A note on eviction data

Eviction filing rates were evaluated as a fourth indicator but could not be included in this version: the Eviction Lab at Princeton no longer provides public bulk downloads for Texas census tracts. A future update will incorporate eviction data from Texas Housers (texashousers.org), which tracks Bexar County filings annually via the Texas Public Information Act.
